06 / Key Takeaways

So what should a content team actually do?

01

Optimize for saves and shares, not just likes

Saves and shares scale up most sharply between the "low" and "viral" buckets (saves: 17.7 → 66.2, shares: 5.9 → 22.5) — both signal content worth returning to or passing on, and both correlate strongly with overall engagement rate.

02

Don't over-engineer hashtags or captions

Engagement is essentially flat across hashtag counts (4–12 tags) and caption lengths (90–150 characters). The only dips appear at the extremes — very short captions (<90 chars) and very long ones (150+), and at 13+ hashtags.

03

CTAs and follower count aren't the lever you think

Explicit calls-to-action show no measurable lift (4.19% vs 4.22% without), and account size barely correlates with engagement rate (r = -0.006) — smaller accounts engage their audiences just as effectively as larger ones.

04

Reels and Fashion/Music/Fitness content have a slight edge

Reels post the highest average engagement of the three formats (4.23%), and Fashion, Music and Fitness content lead the category rankings — modest advantages, but consistent enough to inform a content mix.

05

Posting time is a minor lever — Tue/Fri/Sun edge out

Tuesday, Friday and Sunday post slightly higher average engagement than Wednesday or Monday, and early hours (2–3am, 8am) show small bumps — but the overall spread across the week is under half a percentage point.

06

The real signal is the bucket, not the brief

Format, topic, timing and captions explain only a sliver of the variance. The dominant factor remains whatever drives a post into the "high" or "viral" bucket in the first place — likely creative quality, hook strength, and timing relative to audience activity that this dataset doesn't fully capture.
